The space between things



  • Graphic interventions in
  • five buildings about to be demolished
  • Santiago, Chile, 1998-99
  • - Demolition I (August-September 1998), Avenida El Bosque 0500-512-518, Providencia. Demolition: May 1999
  • - Demolition II (September 1998), Helvetia 261, Providencia. Demolition: May 1999
  • - Demolition III (October 1998), Av. Américo Vespucio Norte 86-64, Las Condes. Demolition: October 1998
  • - Demolition IV (November 1998), Augusto Leguía Norte 100, Las Condes. Demolition: November 1998
  • - Demolition V (December 1998), Pedro de Valdivia 2226, Providencia. Demolition: January 1999

  • Fund for the Development of Culture and Arts, FONDART, 1998

In 1998 I made The Space Between Things, a project in Santiago, Chile. I covered the floors of these abandoned houses and buildings, with thin layers of powdered pigments. More than imposing themselves in the place, these interventions revealed what was already there: the drawing of a wood floor, the outline of a door, forgotten objects. The deposits of pigment occupied the space to produce a discontinuity. Laid out on the floor, they altered and interrupted the transit, one could not circulate without destroying the "drawing". The imprints of the space became the main characters: traces, stains, debris, dust.
